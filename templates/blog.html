Understanding FIBC (Flexible Intermediate Bulk Container)

Flexible Intermediate Bulk Container (FIBC), commonly known as bulk bags, are large, flexible containers used for storing and transporting various materials in bulk quantities. These versatile bags have gained immense popularity across industries due to their efficiency, cost-effectiveness and environmental friendliness.

What is FIBC?

FIBCs are large, woven polypropylene bags designed to handle and transport bulk materials. The safe working load ranges from 500 to 2000 kgs and are known for their durability and strength. These bags are constructed with specialized lifting loops, allowing them to be easily handled by cranes or forklifts.

Benefits of FIBC:

  • Cost-Effective Transportation and Storage

    FIBCs are more cost-effective compared to traditional rigid containers like drums or boxes, as they reduce the need for additional packaging materials and labour.

  • Space Efficiency

    FIBCs are collapsible and can be folded when empty, saving significant storage space in warehouses or during return transportation.

  • Customizable

    FIBCs can be tailored to specific requirements, including size, shape and features like filling spouts, discharge spouts, liners and baffles.

  • Environmentally Friendly

    They are reusable and recyclable, contributing to a reduction in waste generation and carbon footprint.

  • Improved Safety

    Type C and D FIBCs prevent static discharges, reducing the risk of fire or explosion when handling combustible materials.

  • Versatility

    FIBCs can be used for a wide range of materials including powders, granules, flakes and even hazardous materials.

  • Ease of Handling

    Designed with lifting loops, FIBCs can be easily handled by forklifts, cranes or other material handling equipment.

  • Protection from Contamination

    Liners can be added to FIBCs to protect the contents from moisture, UV light or external contaminants.

  • Global Compliance

    FIBCs are manufactured to meet international standards, ensuring they can be used for global trade.

  • Reduced Packaging Waste

    Using FIBCs eliminates the need for single-use, disposable packaging, contributing to a more sustainable supply chain.
